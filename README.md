### Overview of the English Education System

The English education system is organised into four main phases (table) before entry into the labour market:

- Primary Education (Years 1 to 6),
- Secondary Education (Years 7 to 13),
- Higher Education (Bachelor's, Master's and Doctorate).

Compulsory schooling spans from age 5 to 16 - established under **Section 7 of the Education Act 1996**, which places a duty on parents to ensure children of compulsory school age receive efficient full-time education. This was extended by the **Education and Skills Act 2008**, which introduced a duty for all young people in England to participate in education, an apprenticeship, or employment with training until the age of 18 (in force from 2015). The national curriculum is regulated by the **Department for Education (DfE)** and is organised on the basis of **4 Key Stages** for pupils aged 5-16, setting out programmes of study and attainment targets for all subjects - as established by Part 6 of the **Education Act 2002**, which replaced and updated the curriculum provisions of the Education Act 1996. The statutory framework document, **"National Curriculum in England: Framework for Key Stages 1 to 4"** (updated September 2013, revised 2014), is issued by law and defines the programmes of study and attainment targets for all subjects at all Key Stages for maintained schools. [gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-framework-for-key-stages-1-to-4)
